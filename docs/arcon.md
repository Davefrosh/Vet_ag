# INTRODUCTION

## WHEREAS:

* i. The Advertising Regulatory Council of Nigeria Act No. 23 of 2022 establishes the Advertising Regulatory Council of Nigeria (the Council) for advertising, advertisements and marketing communications as the apex regulatory authority for the Nigerian advertising industry, makes provisions for the regulation and control of advertising, ensures the protection of the general public and consumers, promotes local content and entrenches the best international practices;

* ii. Section 8(s) sets out as a function of the Council, to set the standard for good advertising, advertisement and marketing communications in Nigeria including publication and enforcement of codes, guidelines, regulations, proclamations or other subsidiary legislation on content of advertising for sustainable advertising;

* iii. Section 9(b) empowers the Council to authorize, formulate, establish and issue code, proclamations, standards of practice, guidelines, and other regulations on advertising, advertisement and marketing communications in Nigeria;

* iv. Section 61 further reemphasizes that the Council may, subject to the provisions of the Act, issue regulations, codes, standard of practice, proclamations, guidelines, or manuals for the purpose of giving effect to the provisions of the Act and for its administration.

* v. The Honorable Minister of Information and Culture has approved the issuance of this Code in accordance with the

# OBJECTIVE OF THE CODE


---


# CITATION

This Code is made to promote decent advertisement, advertising and marketing communications with the highest ethical standard, consistent with Nigerian's indigenous tenets, morals, diverse culture and local heritage. It also seeks efficient regulation of all aspects of advertisement, advertising and marketing communications directed at the Nigerian market and to complement existing Federal legislations aimed at regulating and controlling advertisements.


---


This Code shall be cited as the Nigerian Code of Advertising.

# SCOPE AND APPLICATION


---


# This Code applies to:

* i. Individual, organization, body corporate or agency of the Federal Government, State or Local Government which engages in, regulates, sponsors or takes benefit of advertising services, advertisements and marketing communications services; and
* ii. Any person who sponsors or takes benefit of an advertising, advertisement or marketing communications services within the provisions of this Act.
* iii. Any person or organization registered, authorised or licensed to practice or carry on or engage in advertisement, advertising and marketing communications practice directed at the Nigerian market;
* iv. Any person or organization in Nigeria that engages in advertisement, advertising and marketing communication whether directed at the Nigerian Market or foreign market.
* v. All sectoral associations recognized by the Council and their members;
* vi. All organizations and individuals that promotes or benefits from the use advertisement, advertising and marketing communications services in Nigeria.

> This Code operates together with all relevant laws in Nigeria, as well as the existing rules and regulations of the Council.

This Code applies to the entire content of an advertisement, advertising and marketing communication, including words and numbers (written or spoken), visual presentations, mark, sign, symbol, smells, music and sound effects or any representation of the acts.


---


# GUIDING PRINCIPLES

All advertisements, advertising and marketing communications in Nigeria or directed at the Nigerian market shall be legal, decent, honest, truthful, respectful and mindful of Nigeria's culture, constitutional tenets and relevant lawful enactments.

They shall be prepared with high sense of social responsibility and shall be devoid of misinformation or disinformation.

All advertisements, advertising and marketing communications shall conform to the principles of fair competition generally accepted in business, and of fair comments. They shall aim to enhance public confidence in advertising and shall be mindful of the interest of the consumer and the public.


---


# PART I – ADVERTISING PRACTICE

## CHAPTER ONE

### THE GENERAL PRINCIPLES

#### Article 1: Legality
All advertisements, advertising and marketing communications shall be legal. They shall conform to all existing laws, rules and regulations in Nigeria.

#### Article 2: Decency
Advertisements, advertising and marketing communications shall not use visual illustrations, words or sounds that offend public decency. Advertisements shall not be obscene or offensive.

#### Article 3: Honesty
(a) All advertisements, advertising and marketing communications shall be prepared in a manner which shall not abuse the trust of consumers or exploit their lack of experience or knowledge.

(b) Factors likely to affect consumers' decisions shall be communicated in a way and in a manner and time that consumers can take them into consideration.

#### Article 4: Exploitation of Sexual Behaviour
Advertisements, advertising and marketing communications shall not exploit, depict, or suggest sexual behaviour in obvious or implied context.

#### Article 5: Gender Discrimination
No gender shall be depicted as subservient, superior or inferior to


---


# Articles 6–12

the other, or as object to satisfy the sexual pleasure of the other.

## Article 6: Superstition
Superstition or myth shall not be promoted, or exploited even if such superstition or myth reflects any aspect of culture, philosophy, or view.

## Article 7: Appeal to Fear and Distress
Advertisements, advertising and marketing communications shall not play on or exploit fear or cause distress.

## Article 8: Offensive Advertisement
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication shall not describe or dramatize distress or a morbid situation in an offensive manner.

## Article 9: Minors and Young Persons
* (a) Special care shall be taken in advertisements, advertising and marketing communications directed at or featuring minors and young persons. Such advertisements, advertising and marketing communications shall not undermine positive social behaviour, lifestyle, and attitude.
* (b) Products unsuitable for minors and young persons shall not be advertised in media targeted at them. Advertisements, advertising and marketing communications directed at minors and young persons shall not be inserted in the media where the subject is unsuitable for minors and young persons.
* (c) Materials unsuitable for minors and young persons shall be clearly identified as such.

## Article 10: Testimonials
Advertisements, advertising and marketing communications containing testimonials and endorsements shall be genuine, and any model used shall be suitable for the product, service, cause or idea endorsed.

## Article 11: Copyright
* (a) Advertisements, advertising and marketing communications shall not contain any item which is in breach of Nigeria and international trademark and copyright laws, or breach any intellectual property law.
* (b) Copyright permission shall be obtained from the original owner or rights holder before any such right may be used.

## Article 12: Privacy
* (a) The right of individuals to privacy shall be respected. Pictures, names, identity and properties of individuals shall not be used in a manner that suggests the individual's personal endorsement without prior consent.
* (b) Proper contractual agreements shall be entered into by the


---


agencies, advertisers, and individuals, and the contractual terms respected by all the parties.

# Article 13: Data Protection

* (a) Personal data of an individual or organisation shall not be included in any advertisement, advertising and marketing communications, sponsorship or promotion without the express consent of the individual or organisation except where the data is part of any historical research or statistical purpose for public interest and the published data is accurate, adequate and without prejudice to the individual or organisation;

* (b) All personal data of any individual or organisation collected for the purpose of advertisement, advertising and marketing communication, sponsorship or promotion shall be:
  - (i) stored only for the period within which it is reasonably needed; and
  - (ii) secured against foreseeable hazard and breach including theft, hacking, cyber-attack, viral attack, dissemination, manipulation, and wilful damage.

* (c) All personal data of an individual or organisation collected for the purpose of advertisement, advertising and marketing communications, sponsorship or promotion shall be with the express consent of the individual or organisation not influenced by fraud, coercion or undue influence;

* (d) An individual or organisation shall have the right to withdraw consent at any time. However, the withdrawal of consent shall not affect any lawful action in the advertisement, advertising and marketing communication exposed before the withdrawal of consent;

* (e) Express consent shall be obtained before a personal data is transferred to a third party;

* (f) Any individual or organisation that violates any provision of this Article shall be liable to a fine provided in Article 148 (f).

# Article 14: Restriction on Data Transfer

* Data collected in Nigeria for the purpose of advertisement, advertising and marketing communication directed at the Nigerian market shall not be transferred outside Nigeria without the approval of Council.

# Article 15: Identity

* (a) Advertisement, advertising and marketing communications soliciting patronage or reaction shall show the identity of the advertiser as well as the contact details where calls can be made to ascertain genuineness of claims in the advertisement.

* (b) No advertiser, Agency or Media organisation shall accept or expose advertisement, advertising and marketing


---


communication which does not indicate proper identity of the sponsor. Teaser advertisement may be exposed provided the Standards Panel and the media organization is provided with details of the advertiser.

(c) Advertisement, advertising and marketing communications shall not bear signoffs such as `committee of friends`, `concerned citizens`, or any other broad terms without verifiable names and addresses.

## Article 16: Camouflage
* (a) Advertisement, advertising and marketing communications shall be clearly distinguished and shall not be disguised as news, editorial matter or other related or similar captions.
* (b) The use of headlines or copies such as `News Flash`, `Breaking News`, and `Public Service Announcement` or any other related or similar captions to introduce or convey an advertisement, advertising or marketing communications is prohibited.

## Article 17: Religion
* (a) No religious belief shall be disparaged or exploited, whether or not to mislead people, or employ phenomenon as a bait to elicit patronage.
* (b) Promotion of a particular faith or belief shall not offend the right of people to hold contrary belief or conviction.
* (c) Offensive or disrespectful references shall not be made of any religion or religious belief.

## Article 18: Instigation of Violence
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communications shall not instigate or encourage violence against people, product, animal or any other object.

## Article 19: Competing Products and Services
Two or more advertisements, advertising and marketing communications for competing or similar products or services shall not be placed adjacent to each other in any media. Violation of the provision of this Article shall be sanctioned in accordance with Article 148(f).

## Article 20: Price
Advertisers and agencies are encouraged to indicate the prices of products, or services in their communications.

## Article 21: Product Hype
* (a) Announcers, On-Air-Personalities, writers and other editorial personnel shall not engage in product hype.
* (b) Any individual or organisation that violates any provision of this Article shall be liable to a fine provided in Article 148(f).


---


# Article 22: Denigration

Denigration of any religion, person, ethnic group, tribe or group of persons, firm, organisation, industry, or commercial activity, profession or product, or any act which seeks to bring such into public contempt or ridicule is prohibited.

# Article 23: Comparative Advertising

* (a) Advertisements, advertising and marketing communications for products and services shall not unfairly discredit, disparage, or attack other products, services, advertisements or companies, or exaggerate the nature or importance of competitive differences.

* (b) Advertisements, advertising and marketing communications for products and services shall not imitate the slogans, illustrations or any form of expression of another advertiser in such manner that may mislead consumers.

# Article 24: Presentation for Approval

* (a) Except for obituary and vacancy, all advertisements, advertising and marketing communications shall be presented for vetting and must obtain the approval of the Standards Panel before exposure. Nothing in this Article shall exempt public service announcement, obituary and vacancy from complying with other standards set out in this Code.

* (b) Strategy for brand event, show, activation or similar undertaking aimed at creating, planning, strategising, managing, developing, producing, propagating, servicing or facilitating an advertisement, brand or marketing communication idea shall be presented for vetting and must obtain the approval of the Standards Panel before exposure.

* (c) It is mandatory for advertiser, advertisement agency, and media house, to obtain the Standards Panel's Certificate of Approval for any advertisement, advertising and marketing communication before the exposure of any such material, and for every media house to demand the Standards Panel's Certificate of Approval in the case of direct media buying before placing any advertisement, advertising and marketing communication.

* (d) Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for vetting shall be presented through registered advertising practitioner in a Council-licensed advertising firm or organisation.

* (e) Where required, application for variation may be made to the Director General for the consideration of any advertisement, advertising or marketing communication for which specified condition have been set by the Council.

* (f) Appeal against a vetting decision of the Standards Panel shall be made to the Director General of the Council.

# CHAPTER TWO

## PRESENTATIONS AND CLAIMS

### Article 25: Erroneous or Misleading Impression
No description, claim or illustration which, expressly or impliedly convey an erroneous or misleading impression about a product or service advertised or about its suitability for any purpose shall be made.

### Article 26: Substantiation
* (a) Any description, claim, illustration, superlative or comparative attribute made in any advertisement, advertising and marketing communication shall be subject to empirical proof and shall be substantiated.
* (b) Proof or substantiation shall be available and shall be produced without delay and upon request to the Advertising Standards Panel.
* (c) Any testimonial or endorsement made in any advertisement, advertising and marketing communication shall be subject to verification.

### Article 27: Consent
* (a) Where testimonial or endorsement in any advertisement, advertising and marketing communications suggest or recommends the use of a product, prior written consent of the model used shall be obtained before the exposure of such advertisement.


---

## (b)
Where the person giving the testimonial or making the endorsement is a minor or young person, prior written consent of the parents or guardians must be obtained.

## Article 28: Scientific Terms and Data
Complex computations and scientific terms or data in a manner to confer authenticity on claims which such complex computations and scientific terms or data do not possess shall not be used.

## Article 29: Statistics
Statistics with limited validity or application in such a way as to make such statistics appear to be universally true or applicable shall not be employed.

## Article 30: Guarantee/Warranty
Words such as `“guarantee”` or `“warranty”` may be used provided that the condition of sale or purchase of the product or service which the word refers to are clearly stated and details of where the terms of `“guarantee”` or `“warranty”` can be examined or studied are clearly indicated before patronage.

## Article 31: Passing-Off
Passing-off or false representation which are likely to confuse or induce a person in such a manner to believe that a product, service or advertisement of one are those of, or is connected with that of another shall not be permitted.

## Article 32: Imitation
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication shall not imitate or be closely similar to another previously published or exposed advertisement to the extent that the latter is confused with, or associated with the former.

## Article 33: Hidden Extras
Indication of any cost or charge for a product or service shall disclose all information relating to the cost or charge of the product or service in such a manner that consumers will not be required to incur extra cost or charge for the product or service.

## Article 34: Bait and Switch Advertising
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communications shall not appear to offer any product or service as not for sale which is intended for sale as a means to switch any consumer to other product or service.

# CHAPTER THREE

## SPECIAL PROVISIONS

### ALCOHOL BEVERAGES

#### Article 35: Exposure in Programmes/Publications
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for alcoholic beverages shall not be exposed in any programme or publication for Minors and Young Persons, religious or sports.

#### Article 36: Prohibited Models
Minors, young persons, sportsmen or sportswomen and pregnant women shall not be used as models in any advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for alcoholic beverages.

#### Article 37: Prohibited Connotation
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for alcoholic beverages shall not use religious or medical suggestions or connotations.

#### Article 38: Outdoor Radius
(a) Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for alcoholic beverages shall not be sited within a radius of 200 meters from any place of worship, hospital, school, or motor parks. However, alcoholic beverage advertisement within the manufacturer's premises or the perimeter wall may be permitted if authorised by the Standards Panel.

(b) Any individual or organisation that violates the provisions of this Article shall be liable to a fine provided in Article 148(f).

## Article 39: Sponsorship & Event
* (a) Notwithstanding any provision of this Code, sponsorship of sporting or entertainment event by alcoholic beverage brand may be permitted where necessary, provided the event is designated for adult audience. However, where such events is covered by, or aired in any broadcast media, only brand name, corporate name, logo and/or product pack may be deployed.
* (b) Event and competition directed at minors and young persons shall not be linked to any alcoholic beverage through sponsorship or any other form of marketing support.
* (c) Any individual or organisation that violates any provision of this Article shall be liable to a fine provided in Article 148(f).

## Article 40: Gift Items
* (a) Gift items for alcoholic beverage shall not be directed at minors, young persons, sportsmen/women and pregnant women.
* (b) Any individual or organisation that violates the provision of this Article shall be liable to a fine provided in Article 148(f).

## Article 41: Claims, Insinuations and Associations
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for any alcoholic beverage shall not -
* (a) suggest or imply that the alcoholic beverage has health benefits;
* (b) be associated with piloting, driving, riding or any extreme sports activity;
* (c) display any person who states that he/she drank alcoholic beverage as a minor or young person;
* (d) display any person dressed in any apparel associated with healthcare professions.

## Article 42: Time of Exposure
* (a) Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for alcoholic beverage shall not be aired between 6:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m. on radio and between 6:00 a.m. and 10:00 p.m. on television and between 6:00 a.m. and 10:00 p.m. on any outdoor platform which displays motion or moving picture.
* (b) Any individual or organisation that violates any provision of this Article shall be liable to a fine provided in Article 148(f).

## Article 43: Portrayal of Irresponsible Drinking
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for alcoholic beverage shall not portray people drinking irresponsibly or implying that such behaviour is attractive or appropriate.


---


# Article 44: Age Limit of Models
Models used in advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for alcoholic beverage shall not appear to be less than 21 years old.

# Article 45: Social Acceptance
* (a) Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication shall not imply that alcoholic beverage consumption is essential for business or social success or acceptance;
* (b) Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication shall not portray as undesirable abstinence from alcoholic drink or suggest it is wrong or socially unacceptable to decline an offer of alcoholic drink.

# Article 46: Sexual Indulgence
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for alcoholic beverage shall not present or imply acts of sexual indulgence or its permissiveness or portray nudity or any form of indecency;

# Article 47: Attributes
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for alcoholic beverage shall not offer the product as a stimulant, sedative or tranquillizer or that its consumption can improve performance.

# TOBACCO

## Article 48: Prohibition of Tobacco Advertising
* (a) Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for tobacco product in any form or manner is prohibited.
* (b) Any individual or organisation which violates the provision of this Article shall be liable to a fine of not less than N3,000,000.00K (Three Million Naira).

## Article 49: Prohibition of Sponsorship
* (a) All forms of brand sponsorship, consumer engagement and brand communication for tobacco product is prohibited.
* (b) Any individual or organization which violates the provision of this Article shall be liable to a fine of not less than N3,000,000.00K (three million Naira).

# CONDOM

## Article 50: Indecent Use
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for condom shall not encourage indecency in its use. Emphasis in the communication shall be focused on health and family planning applications.


---


# Article 51: Insinuations
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for condom shall not dramatize or in any other manner depict or insinuate the sexual act by word, graphic, sound or any other action.

# Article 52: Health Warning
Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for condom shall carry health warning that states "Condom is not 100 per cent safe. Total abstinence or faithfulness is the best option" or any other health warning as may be directed by the Federal Ministry of Health.

# Article 53: Exposure of Condom Advertisement
(a) Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for condom shall not –
* (i) Be aired during minor and young persons' programmes;
* (ii) Be aired between 6:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m. on radio and between 6:00 a.m. and 10:00 p.m. on television and between 6:00 a.m. and 10:00 p.m. on any outdoor platform which displays motion or moving pictures;
* (iii) Be sited near school or place of worship.

(b) Any individual or organisation that violates any provision of this Article shall be liable to a fine provided in Article 148 (f).

# GAMBLING

## Article 54: Gambling Activity
(a) Advertiser or promoter of gambling activity shall comply with relevant laws relating to lottery, competition, prize draw, instant-win offer, premium promotion etc.

(b) Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for gambling activity shall not
* (i) portray, condone or encourage gambling behavior which is socially irresponsible or which could lead to financial, social or emotional harm or exploit the susceptibilities, credulity, inexperience or lack of knowledge of a vulnerable person;
* (ii) suggest that gambling can provide an escape from personal, professional or educational problem, loneliness or depression or suggest that gambling can be a solution to financial concerns or an alternative to employment or a way to achieve financial security;
* (iii) suggest that participating in lottery can enhance personal qualities or improve self-image or self-esteem or is a way to gain control, superiority, recognition or admiration;
* (iv) be exposed in children, religious and sports programmes;

# Health, Beauty Product and Therapy

## Article 56: Diet and Lifestyle

* (a) Advertisement, advertising and marketing communication for health, beauty product and therapy shall not disparage good dietary practice and shall avoid anything likely to encourage poor nutritional habits or unhealthy lifestyle, especially among children.

* (b) Nutrition or health claims shall be supported by scientific evidence.

* (c) Generalized claims such as 'goodness', 'wholesome' or any similar claim shall not exaggerate the nutritional or health benefits of food product or any ingredient.

* (d) Reference to the properties of any ingredient shall not give misleading impression of the properties of the whole product.

* (e) The scientific meaning of any word shall not be confused with its colloquial meaning or physical potency.

* (a) Promoter shall be able to demonstrate that reasonable estimate of likely responses and provisions are made to meet demand.
* (b) Phrase such as `subject to availability` shall not relieve promoter of any obligation to take reasonable steps to cover participant's rightful claim.
* (c) Promoter shall not encourage consumer to make any purchase or series of purchases as a precondition to applying for promotional items where the number of those items is limited.
* (d) Where promoters are unable to meet the demand for a promotional offer because of unexpectedly high response or any other unanticipated factor beyond their control, they shall offer refunds or substituted products as may be prescribed by relevant authorities.

## Article 140: Administration of Promotion Activity
* (a) Promotion shall be conducted with proper supervision and adequate resources shall be made available to administer them.

(e) Promoter shall not charge for packaging, handling or basic administration of any promotional material.

## Article 141: Free Offers and Free Trials

* (a) Promoter shall not try to recover cost by reducing the quality or composition or by inflating the price of any product to be bought as a pre-condition of obtaining any free item.

* (b) Promoter shall not describe a component or item in a package as “free” where the cost of that component or item is included in the package price.

* (c) Promoter shall not use the term “free trial” to describe “satisfaction or your money back” offer, “buy one, get one free” offer or other similar offers where a non-refundable purchase is required. However, promoter shall provide a cash refund, postal order, money transfer or personal cheque promptly to free trial participants.

* (d) Consumer's liability for costs shall be made clear in any material showing “free” offers. An offer shall be described as free only if consumers pay no more than the minimum cost of responding to the promotion or the true cost of freight or delivery or the cost of incidental expenses.

## Article 142: Promoter's Due Diligence

* Promoter shall ensure that due diligence is taken prior to embarking on any promotion campaign.

## Article 143: Significant Conditions for Promotions

* (a) Promotion shall specify clearly before any purchase or at the time of entry/application, if no purchase is required:
  - (i) How to participate, including significant conditions, costs, and any other factor reasonably likely to influence consumer's decision or understanding about the promotion;
  - (ii) If any proof of purchase is required;
  - (iii) The minimum number and nature of any prize, if applicable and if prize is to be given in instalments or to be shared among recipients;
  - (iv) Geographical, personal or technological restrictions, age, access to the Internet, etc;
  - (v) Where there is need to obtain permission before participation;
  - (vi) Commencement date of any competition referring to any special offer where the special offer has not begun;
  - (vii) Closing date, where applicable, for purchase or submission of entries/claims.

## Article 144: Other Rules for Prize Promotions

* (a) Promoters shall not claim that consumer have won any prize if consumer has not.

* (b) The distinction between prizes and gifts shall always be clear. Gifts offered to all or most consumers in a promotion shall not be described as prizes.

* (c) Where promoter offers gifts to all or most consumer in addition to giving prizes to prize winners, particular care must be taken to avoid confusing the two categories.

* (d) Promoters shall not overstate consumer's chances of winning a prize. Where promoters include list of consumers who have not won prizes in lists of those who have won prizes, they shall distinguish clearly between the two categories.

* (e) Promoter shall not claim that consumer is lucky by use terms such as `“finalist”` or `“final stage”` in a way that implies that consumer have progressed by chance or skill to an advanced stage of promotions if the consumer had not.

* (f) Promoter shall not claim that consumer shall respond within a specified date or within a specified time if consumer does not need to respond within the period.

## Article 147: Trade Incentives

* (a) Incentive schemes shall be designed and implemented to take account of the interests of persons involved and shall not compromise the obligations of employees to give honest advice to consumers.
* (b) Where promoter intend to seek help from, or offer incentives to another company's employees, promoter shall observe any procedure established by the company for their employees, including any rule for participating in promotion.
* (c) Incentive schemes shall make it clear to those benefiting that the beneficiary may be responsible for paying tax or any other levy as may be demanded by appropriate authority.
* (d) Promoter shall not claim that products would be able to facilitate winning where they are not.
